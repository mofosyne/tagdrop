Multi-Code Cache (Manifest + Chunks)

Content too large for one QR (> ~800 bytes) is split into a Manifest code plus several Chunk codes. Chunks can be scanned in any order — even from different physical locations — and TagDrop reassembles and checksums them once complete.

Sunset Trail story — text/plain, 1426 bytes total, 3 chunks
Cache ID: 81d3adcdf38c1d15
SHA-256 (assembled): 81d3adcdf38c1d15dbb4e9cd3cd554a895d9ae14bf0fccd9da5e67e85d5551fa
① Scan the manifest — any time, before or after the chunks.
② Scan each chunk in any order. TagDrop reassembles and verifies the SHA-256 once all 3 are collected.

Paper Set (Multi-file)

A paper groups multiple files under one manifest. Scan the manifest QR first, then the file QRs in any order.

② Scan each file QR in any order to cache its content.

Multi-Location Trail (Multiple Papers)

Three independent paper manifests — one per "stop" — each with its own icon and related hints to its neighbours. Useful for testing what happens when multiple pages are scanned at different locations: separate map pins, shared collection grouping, and trail navigation hints.

Collection: City Park Trail (#citypark2026)
Collection ID: 7061726b74726c30
① Scan each stop's manifest and index QR — ideally at a different physical location per stop (or with a mock-location app), so each gets its own recorded position.
② Open the Map tab — each stop appears as its own pin, using its icon; zoom in to see the labels.
③ Open the Collections tab — all three stops group together as "City Park Trail" because they share a collection_id, even though each is an independent paper with its own set/slug and related hints to its neighbours.
