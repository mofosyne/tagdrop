- New: set a known location and an "uncertainty radius" on a code's content, with the option to have it take priority over your live GPS — useful when you already know exactly where it's being placed. Shown on the Map tab as a circle.
- Paper directories can now show a short description for the paper and for each file inside it, so you know what something is before opening it.
- Paper layouts too big for one QR code now split automatically across several, just like oversized files already did.
- Optional parity recovery code: when sharing or printing content split across multiple QR codes, you can add one extra code that lets a scanner reconstruct any single missing part.
- Codes can now carry a title and longer description, shown in your collection and history instead of just a filename.
- Content can reply to other content, like a comment thread: open a reply to see what it's responding to, or view all of a code's replies.
- Scan any QR code — contact card, Wi-Fi network, calendar event, and more — even if it isn't a TagDrop code; these get a sensible title instead of "Untitled" and can be reopened later just like any other scan.
- Search your collection by title, hint, or #hashtag, and tap a tag chip to quick-filter to just that tag.
- Back up all your scans, papers, and keys to a single file, and restore them on this or another device.
- Write any TagDrop code straight to an NFC tag, and read NFC tags the same way you scan a QR code.
- New "View as" option on any opened item: switch between the normal rendered view, its decoded text, a hex dump, or its underlying CBOR structure.
- Security: closed a hole that let other apps hijack TagDrop's page viewer with an arbitrary link, and fixed a flaw that could let a malicious code overwrite an unrelated paper you'd already saved.
- Stronger integrity checks on codes split across multiple QR parts.
